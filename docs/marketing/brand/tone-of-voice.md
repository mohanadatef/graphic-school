# Tone of Voice

## Overview

This document defines the tone of voice for all Graphic School 2.0 communications, ensuring consistent messaging across all channels.

## Brand Personality

### Core Traits

1. **Professional yet Approachable**
   - Knowledgeable but not condescending
   - Expert but accessible
   - Confident but humble

2. **Clear and Direct**
   - No jargon without explanation
   - Straightforward communication
   - Easy to understand

3. **Helpful and Supportive**
   - Focus on customer success
   - Proactive assistance
   - Empathetic to challenges

4. **Modern and Innovative**
   - Forward-thinking
   - Technology-forward
   - Contemporary language

5. **Trustworthy and Reliable**
   - Honest communication
   - Transparent about capabilities
   - Dependable messaging

## Voice Characteristics

### Do's

✅ **Use:**
- Clear, simple language
- Active voice
- Specific examples
- Benefit-focused messaging
- Positive framing

**Examples:**
- "Get started in 30 minutes"
- "Save 10 hours per month"
- "Everything you need in one platform"

### Don'ts

❌ **Avoid:**
- Technical jargon (without explanation)
- Passive voice
- Vague claims
- Feature-focused only
- Negative framing

**Examples to Avoid:**
- "Leverage our robust infrastructure" (too jargon-heavy)
- "Mistakes were made" (passive voice)
- "It's good" (too vague)

## Communication Styles by Channel

### Website

**Tone:** Professional, clear, benefit-focused

**Style:**
- Headlines: Clear value propositions
- Body: Easy to scan, benefit-driven
- CTAs: Action-oriented

**Example:**
"Transform your educational institution with a complete learning management platform. Get started in 30 minutes."

### Sales Materials

**Tone:** Confident, helpful, solution-focused

**Style:**
- Address pain points
- Show benefits
- Provide proof
- Clear next steps

**Example:**
"Tired of managing multiple tools? Graphic School 2.0 brings everything together in one platform, saving you 10+ hours per month."

### Support Documentation

**Tone:** Helpful, clear, instructional

**Style:**
- Step-by-step guidance
- Clear explanations
- Examples included
- Troubleshooting tips

**Example:**
"To create a course, follow these steps: 1) Click 'Create Course', 2) Fill in the details, 3) Click 'Save'. That's it!"

### Email Communications

**Tone:** Friendly, professional, concise

**Style:**
- Clear subject lines
- Scannable content
- Action-oriented
- Personal but professional

**Example:**
"Hi [Name], I wanted to follow up on our conversation. Here's how Graphic School can help you [specific benefit]."

### Social Media

**Tone:** Engaging, friendly, informative

**Style:**
- Conversational
- Value-driven
- Visual content
- Community-focused

**Example:**
"Did you know you can set up Graphic School in just 30 minutes? No technical knowledge required. Try it free: [link]"

## Messaging Principles

### 1. Customer-First

Always focus on customer benefits, not just features.

**Feature:** "Multi-language support"  
**Benefit:** "Reach students in their language, increasing engagement and accessibility"

### 2. Clarity Over Cleverness

Be clear rather than clever.

**Clever (Avoid):** "Revolutionize your educational ecosystem"  
**Clear (Use):** "Manage all your courses, students, and content in one platform"

### 3. Specific Over Vague

Use specific numbers and examples.

**Vague:** "Save time"  
**Specific:** "Save 10 hours per month on administrative tasks"

### 4. Positive Framing

Frame messages positively.

**Negative:** "No more complicated systems"  
**Positive:** "Simple, intuitive platform that just works"

### 5. Proof Over Promises

Back up claims with proof.

**Promise:** "Fast performance"  
**Proof:** "Page loads in under 2 seconds, 99.9% uptime"

## Language Guidelines

### Word Choice

**Preferred Words:**
- Simple, easy, clear
- Help, support, guide
- Fast, efficient, streamlined
- Flexible, adaptable, customizable

**Words to Avoid:**
- Complex, complicated, difficult
- Force, require, must
- Slow, inefficient, cumbersome
- Rigid, fixed, limited

### Sentence Structure

**Keep It Simple:**
- Short sentences (15-20 words)
- One idea per sentence
- Active voice
- Clear subject-verb-object

### Paragraph Structure

**Scannable:**
- Short paragraphs (3-4 sentences)
- Clear topic sentences
- Bullet points for lists
- White space for readability

## Examples by Context

### Product Description

**Good:**
"Graphic School 2.0 is a complete learning management platform that helps educational institutions manage courses, students, and content in one place. With flexible course structures, integrated public website, and modern technology, it's the ideal solution for institutions of any size."

**Why It Works:**
- Clear value proposition
- Specific benefits
- Easy to understand
- Benefit-focused

### Feature Description

**Good:**
"Create multiple groups within each course, allowing you to run parallel classes. Assign different instructors to each group, set individual capacities, and manage schedules independently—all while sharing the same course content."

**Why It Works:**
- Explains what it does
- Shows how it helps
- Specific and clear
- Benefit-focused

### Support Message

**Good:**
"I understand you're having trouble with [issue]. Let's fix this together. Here's a step-by-step guide that should help: [steps]. If you're still stuck, just reply and I'll help you personally."

**Why It Works:**
- Empathetic
- Helpful
- Clear instructions
- Supportive

## Cultural Considerations

### International Audience

- Avoid idioms and slang
- Use clear, simple language
- Consider cultural context
- Provide translations when needed

### Technical Audience

- Can use technical terms (with explanation)
- Provide technical details
- Show architecture/technology
- Still keep it clear

### Non-Technical Audience

- Avoid technical jargon
- Use analogies
- Focus on benefits
- Simple explanations

## Consistency Checklist

Before publishing any content, check:

- [ ] Does it match our brand personality?
- [ ] Is it clear and easy to understand?
- [ ] Is it benefit-focused?
- [ ] Is it positive and helpful?
- [ ] Does it sound like us?
- [ ] Is it appropriate for the channel?
- [ ] Does it respect the audience?

## Conclusion

Our tone of voice is:
- **Professional yet approachable**
- **Clear and direct**
- **Helpful and supportive**
- **Modern and innovative**
- **Trustworthy and reliable**

This tone ensures we communicate effectively with our target audience while maintaining a consistent brand identity across all channels.
